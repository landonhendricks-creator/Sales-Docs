Objectives

What Whitney Young Health is trying to accomplish
  • Replace Nextiva with a unified UCaaS + CCaaS platform that serves all roles — CC scheduling agents, clinical nursing staff, and general office users — under one system across 6 locations.
  • Reduce patient hold times and abandoned calls through better queue management, callback capability, and an AI-powered IVR that replaces a complex, confusing phone tree.
  • Give nursing staff a flexible, mobile-friendly way to manage shared voicemails with transcription — visible and actionable by the whole team, not trapped on individual phones.
  • Deliver proactive patient outreach via SMS and voice — appointment reminders, provider callout notifications, and gap list campaigns — without manual intervention.
  • Support 30+ patient languages through real-time AI translation in the contact center, replacing the current manual language line process.
  • Integrate with Athena Health (EHR) to push call summaries and recordings to patient charts, and with Relevant (analytics platform) via API for unified data reporting.
♟️

Strategies

How Whitney Young Health plans to get there
  • Evaluate a single-platform vendor that handles both UC and CC natively — avoid stitching together separate products (Nextiva for UC, third-party for CC) that don't share data or reporting.
  • Use a mixed licensing model — full CC agent seats for scheduling staff, Engage (mobile/informal) for clinical nurses, standard UC for office users — to control cost while meeting all workflows.
  • Prioritize out-of-box AI capability (voice bot, translation, proactive outreach) that can be stood up without a developer — clinical and ops teams don't have the bandwidth for custom builds.
  • Replace the current DTMF phone tree with a voice-enabled AI IVR that lets patients say what they need in their own language and get routed correctly — reducing misroutes and caller frustration.
  • Leverage existing Microsoft E3 licensing — Teams cross-launch integration allows users to stay in a familiar interface while gaining full 8x8 telephony capabilities without re-licensing.

Challenges

Current pain + cost of doing nothing

Current Pain

  • Long patient hold times — no callback option today. Patients wait, get frustrated, or abandon. No data on why calls are abandoned or how long waits are.
  • Nurses physically dialing into individual voicemail boxes to listen to messages one at a time — no transcription, no shared visibility, no way to see what's been handled.
  • Complex DTMF phone tree causing patients to get lost or misrouted. Voice-enabled routing doesn't exist today — callers must navigate press-1, press-2 menus across multiple departments and locations.
  • 30+ patient languages handled through manual language lines — agent places a separate call, merges it, waits. No AI translation in the queue. Slow, inconsistent, adds to handle time.
  • No proactive outreach capability — appointment reminders, provider callouts, and gap list campaigns are manual or don't happen. Provider callout at 7AM means staff scrambling to notify patients individually.
  • Phone directory requires device reboot to reflect updates — new staff additions don't propagate to endpoint directories automatically, creating friction and outdated contact lists across 6 sites.

Cost of Doing Nothing

  • Abandoned calls = missed patient access. For a federally qualified health center serving uninsured and underinsured patients, every dropped call is a missed care opportunity — directly counter to WYH's mission.
  • Nursing staff continues to lose time on voicemail management instead of patient care. Shared queues going unmonitored means messages sit, get missed, or get actioned by the wrong person.
  • No outbound outreach means no-show rates stay elevated and gap list patients go unreached. Revenue impact on both sides — missed appointments and untouched at-risk populations.
  • Manual language line process adds minutes per call and depends on interpreter availability. Patients with limited English face longer waits and less consistent care access than English-speaking patients.
  • No call-to-chart data in Athena means supervisors can't close the loop between patient conversations and clinical records. QA and complaint resolution requires manual hunting across disconnected systems.

Future State & Positive Business Outcomes

Where Whitney Young Health wants to be — measurable value
  • Patients call in, say what they need in any language, get routed to the right department or location without navigating a multi-level DTMF tree — reducing misroutes, hold times, and abandoned calls.
  • Nurses see a shared, transcribed voicemail inbox for their group — read at a glance, flagged when actioned, accessible from laptop or mobile. No more physical phone pickups to sort messages.
  • Provider calls out at 7AM — every affected patient gets an automated SMS or voice message within minutes. No staff scramble, no missed notifications, confirmation or reschedule reply routes to a live agent.
  • Supervisors monitor call quality with sentiment analysis and QMSA — complaints can be reviewed with full call journey, per-segment recordings, and transcriptions. QA goes from reactive to systematic.
  • Every call summary and recording link pushed directly to the Athena patient chart — clinicians have full context before follow-up, no manual documentation step for the agent.
  • 30+ languages served through Crisp real-time AI translation — patient and agent speak naturally, translation is instantaneous. Language access is consistent, fast, and no longer dependent on interpreter line availability.
  • All UC, CC, Engage, and SMS data flows into one unified data lake — Relevant analytics platform receives a clean API feed, eliminating siloed reporting and giving leadership a single view of patient communication performance.
