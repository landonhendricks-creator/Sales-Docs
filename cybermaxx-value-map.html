Objectives

What CyberMaxx is trying to accomplish
  • Deploy a dedicated contact center for ~65 SOC analysts handling inbound customer support calls — without replacing Teams telephony for the remaining ~115 users.
  • Gain out-of-box analytics and reporting on queue performance — including abandoned call root-cause analysis — to answer the six specific business problem statements presented to IT.
  • Implement skills-based routing to separate Tier 1 and Tier 2 SOC analyst queues for more efficient call handling and faster resolution.
  • Maintain Call Cabinet (Smarsh) compliance recording without disruption or re-procurement.
  • Select a vendor by beginning of Q3 2026 and go live by end of Q3 / early Q4 — ahead of a likely H2 acquisition that could bring RingCentral into the org.
♟️

Strategies

How CyberMaxx plans to get there
  • Evaluate Microsoft Teams-certified CCaaS vendors only — Joseph's explicit requirement. SOC analysts must stay in the Teams ecosystem. No separate softphone client, no dual-presence conflicts.
  • Use the six business problem statements as the evaluation filter — function parity against what RingCentral CX claims to solve. Any vendor that doesn't check all six boxes is disqualified.
  • Prioritize cost efficiency — avoid double-licensing (Teams phone + CC UC layer) that RingCentral forces. IT leadership has direct visibility into RC pricing from prior relationship (~$121/user/mo previously).
  • Leverage existing vendor approval — 8x8 is already in CyberMaxx's procurement system from prior relationship, shortcutting vetting process considerably.
  • Move before the H2 acquisition — selecting a CC platform now lets CyberMaxx rationalize any acquired company's RC licenses rather than being absorbed into RingCentral post-deal.

Challenges

Current pain + cost of doing nothing

Current Pain

  • Teams provides near-zero CC telemetry — abandoned calls are happening but Joseph can't determine if they're wrong-number hangs or wait-time dropoffs. Business is demanding answers he can't provide.
  • No skills-based routing — Tier 1 and Tier 2 SOC analysts sit in a flat FIFO queue. Complex calls consume Tier 1 time; escalation is manual and undocumented.
  • Supervisor tools exist (Teams Queues App) but go unused — managers either don't know about listen/whisper features or avoid the interface entirely. Coaching is reactive.
  • Microsoft roadmap is unreliable for CC features — user feedback is ignored, new releases are minimally viable. Joseph has stopped waiting.

Cost of Doing Nothing

  • If H2 acquisition brings RingCentral into the org with no CC vendor already selected, CyberMaxx loses platform control — they absorb RC rather than rationalize it out.
  • Business stakeholders continue pushing RingCentral CX without a credible alternative — RC wins by default. Cost for 65 agents would approach what CyberMaxx paid for the entire company historically.
  • RingCentral's forced UC bundling forces double-licensing on top of existing M365 E5 — a structural cost problem Joseph has already identified as a non-starter.
  • SOC analysts continue working blind — no performance data, no coaching tools, no abandoned call visibility. Customer experience gaps compound and go unmeasured.

Future State & Positive Business Outcomes

Where CyberMaxx wants to be — measurable value
  • SOC analysts remain fully inside the Teams ecosystem — no dual-client friction, no presence conflicts, seamless internal transfers without switching apps.
  • Out-of-box reporting answers all six business problems — cradle-to-grave call journey, abandoned call root-cause, real-time queue health, and historical SLA performance available without custom builds or IT overhead.
  • Tier 1 / Tier 2 skills-based routing reduces average handle time and escalation noise — analysts work the right calls, supervisors have live data to intervene proactively, not reactively.
  • Call Cabinet integration maintained with zero disruption — compliance recording continues without re-procurement, re-certification, or workflow changes.
  • Vendor selected before H2 acquisition — CyberMaxx enters post-acquisition integration with a CC platform already contracted, giving leverage to rationalize or eliminate acquired company's RC licenses.
  • Future-ready for AI expansion — unified data lake means keyword alerting, speech analytics, and omnichannel can be added when the business asks, without rebuilding reporting infrastructure from scratch.
